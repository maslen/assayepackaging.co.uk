Laminated Pouches

Laminated means layered. A laminated pouch is therefore made up of different layers of material which are chosen for their individual properties.

Most laminated pouches consist of PET or OPP which is the outer layer. This plastic material gives very good printability, total or partial matt finish and a high or medium melting temperature.

The inner layer usually consists of PE which is thermosealable, has good hot tack for large bags, a combination of LLDPE/MDPE, white content for opacity and coextrusion.

The inner and outer layers are laminated together to produce a reel of printed film which can be made into pre-formed pouches or supplied on the reel to a V.F.F.S. Packaging Machine.

There are many different types of laminated pouches, in all shapes and sizes - Block Bottom, Stand Up, Doy Pack and Vacuum Pack to name a few.

Features include:

  • Reseal - Zipper
  • Tear Notches / Laser Cut
  • Euro-Slot
  • Rounded Corners
